Frequently asked questions +
How is this different from asking ChatGPT?+

Moodwatch is built around how you feel right now, not a generic prompt. It reads your Letterboxd watchlist, takes mood and runtime into account, and surfaces films from a curated catalogue of 1,500+ titles plus TMDb. ChatGPT doesn't know what you saved, what you've already watched, or where to actually stream it tonight.

Are the streaming suggestions accurate?+

We pull streaming data from JustWatch via TMDb. Availability changes weekly and varies by country. The "Where to watch" link always opens the right page, but if a service just dropped a title, you might catch it before our cache refreshes. When in doubt, click through.

What does the Letterboxd integration do?+

If you give us your @, we read your public watchlist and prioritize films you already saved. We never recommend something you've already logged as watched. Your username isn't stored — we resolve it on each call and cache the resolved film IDs anonymously.

Why is the watchlist slow the first time?+

Letterboxd doesn't expose film IDs directly, so we resolve them in small batches and cache the result. The first call covers part of your watchlist; the next two or three reloads cover the rest. After that it's instant for a week.

Do you save my data?+

No accounts, no tracking, no email. Liked and disliked picks live in your browser's localStorage — clear them with "Reset my taste" at the bottom. The Letterboxd cache holds anonymized film IDs only, never your username.

Is it really free?+

Yes. No paywall, no ads, no upsell. If you want to keep it alive, there's a Ko-fi link at the bottom. That's it.

How are the picks generated?+

A short ritual asks about your mood, energy, runtime and risk appetite. Those answers map to TMDb genre filters and a curated list of editor picks. The scorer mixes ratings, mood fit, watchlist overlap and a small surprise factor so you don't get the same six films twice.
